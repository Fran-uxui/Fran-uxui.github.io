Process.

Research.

I start taking notes on what are the objectives of the product, and what are the user objectives. With this information I take a look at similar products and their design over the user behavior.

Brainstorm.

In this step I think of various funnels for the desired actions of the client (Like buy, download, leave mail…). Then comes the visual part, I start with the color palette, font, borders, and the main order of things.

Unify Concepts.

With everything I gathered and designed already, here I make a simple guide of the important things for the sketch. Usually this step is made in a pdf with references and the best ideas.

Sketch.

In the sketch step I made more than one sketch, depending on the complexity of the project and the amount of content, this is made on paper, but sometimes I make it digitally.

Wireframe.

Here the final concept is made, taking concepts from the different sketches, with the colors and fonts chosen in the brainstorm step. This is made in a design software like illustrator or figma, and sent for revision.

Tech Research.

Now I know what i have to take from the wireframe and make it to the final website/app, usually there are various ways to do this, depending on the client necessities (if i use a framework and the javascript libraries that i will need).

Coding.

I make first the html, css and then add everything that was defined in the step before this.

Debug + Polish.

Taking a look in various ways the code I made can be improved, sometimes there are responsive problems, and I have to adjust the css for different screen sizes and if it's portrait screen or landscape.

About me.

Hi! My name is Fran, I'm a UX/UI designer and front-end developer for the web. Born in 99’ I started designing at 12 years old as a hobby, then at 16 I studied photography and developed a professional career in photography and video production while doing designs for companies in my town. With the covid-19 pandemic (2020) the photography industry suffered a lot (specially the social event photography, where i was working) and I started studying front-end web development with a few personal projects, then a design studio needed my set of skills for their clients, that's where my design and developer career took a big step, and i learned a lot in the last years. Here I am, at the start of a long journey, making my set of skills bigger every day! while adding value to the companies I work for.
